We are committed to sparking curiosity in future engineers and explorers. Through interactive STEM workshops, school visits, and community showcases, the Queen's HERC Design Team engages with students of all ages to share the excitement of rover design and planetary exploration. Our mission goes beyond the competition—we aim to inspire the next generation of problem solvers and innovators through hands-on learning and storytelling.
Innovation starts with intentional design. At Queen’s, we approach rover development with creativity, engineering rigor, and systems thinking. From custom non-pneumatic wheel fabrication to modular frame optimization, our design process is guided by data, collaboration, and real-world constraints modeled after NASA's Artemis mission goals. Each decision we make is grounded in functionality, sustainability, and the pursuit of engineering excellence.
We bring concepts to life through student-led fabrication and prototyping. Every component of our rover—from drivetrain to suspension—is built in-house by our undergraduate team using campus facilities and industry-standard tools. The build phase is where our design takes shape, requiring hands-on problem-solving, strict adherence to safety, and adaptability in the face of unexpected challenges. We build with purpose, precision, and pride.
The race is where everything comes together. Competing on a simulated extraterrestrial terrain, our rover must overcome technical obstacles, real-time mission decisions, and strict time constraints. With two pilots and a unified team behind them, the Queen’s HERC rover pushes the limits of student-built engineering. It's not just a test of hardware—it's a demonstration of teamwork, resilience, and our readiness to take on the future of space exploration.
